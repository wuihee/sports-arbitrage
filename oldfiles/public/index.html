What is Sports Arbitrage?

*Sports arbitrage is a strategy that involves placing bets on all possible outcomes of a sporting event to guarantee a profit regardless of the result.

These opportunities present themselves because different bookmakers offer different odds for the same sporting event. And we can take advantage of this discrepancy to make free money.

Example

Let's say there is a tennis match between Player A and Player B.

  • Bookmaker 1 offers odds of 2.10 on Player A.
  • Bookmaker 2 offers odds of 2.10 on Player B.

By placing a bet of $100 on Player A with Bookmaker 1 and a bet of $100 on Player B with Bookmaker 2, you are guaranteed a return of $210 no matter who wins, which gives you a profit of $10 on your $200 total bet.

Limitations

However, it is not easy to arb. Betting odds move very quickly making it hard to place bets consistently. In addition, bookmakers hate arbing and will quickly ban you when they realize what you are doing.

How Does This Work?

Real-time data on sporting odds can be retrieved from the Odds API. For each sporting event, we search for arbitrage opportunities by finding the maximum odds offered by all available bookmakers. We can calculate the arbitrage percentage by taking the sum of the reciprocal of the odds and if this percentage is less than 1, we have an arbitrage.
